## LAYER 0: ZERO PREP — YOU HAVE NOTHING
### *Grid is down. No generator. No solar. How do you get electricity RIGHT NOW?*

### YOUR CAR IS A GENERATOR
**Every vehicle is a power plant. Most people don't realize this.**
- Car battery: 12V, typically 50-70 Ah = 600-840 Wh of stored energy
- That's enough to charge 30+ phones, run LED lights for days, power a radio for weeks
- **Add a power inverter** ($30-50 for 1000W pure sine wave) and your car battery outputs standard 120V AC
- Run the engine 15-30 min/hour to recharge the battery. 1 gallon of gas = hours of intermittent power.
- **DO NOT drain the battery below 50%** or you can't restart the engine. Monitor voltage — 12.4V = 50%, stop there.

**Parts list — make your car a generator:**
| Item | Cost | What It Does |
|------|------|-------------|
| 1000W pure sine wave inverter | $30-50 | Converts 12V DC to 120V AC. Plug in anything. |
| 12V to USB adapter (4-port) | $10 | Charge 4 phones/devices simultaneously |

| LED lantern (rechargeable USB) | $10-15 | 200+ hours of light from one charge |
| **TOTAL** | **$70-105** | **Turns any car into a power station** |

### WOOD STOVE = FREE ELECTRICITY
**If you're already burning wood for heat, you're wasting electricity.**
A thermoelectric generator (TEG) converts the temperature difference between the hot stove surface and cool air into electricity. No moving parts. No fuel beyond what you're already burning.

| Product | Watts | Cost | What It Powers |
|---------|-------|------|---------------|
| Devil Watt 15W (air-cooled) | 15W | $300 | Phone charging, LED lights, radio |
| **Devil Watt 45W (air-cooled)** | 45W | $700 | Lights + phones + laptop + mesh radio + router |
| Devil Watt 100W (water-cooled) | 100W | $1,200 | Small appliance level — fridge, tools intermittently |

**The 45W unit is the sweet spot:** No plumbing (air-cooled), sits on your stove pipe, generates power the entire time you're heating. 45W continuous = 1+ kWh/day while the stove runs. That's lights, phones, radios, a laptop — the digital essentials — for free from heat you're already making.

### HAND CRANK AND MUSCLE POWER
- **Hand-crank generator** (K-TOR Pocket Socket 2): $65, generates 120V AC at 10W by cranking. 1 hour = 1 full phone charge.
- **Bicycle generator** (Pedal Power Plus): $300-500. Pedaling at moderate effort = 50-100W. 1 hour pedaling = charge a laptop.
- **Reality check:** Human power is a last resort. 100W sustained effort is exhausting. It works for charging small devices, not running appliances.

### SINGLE SOLAR PANEL — THE $100 POWER PLANT
- One 200W solar panel: $80-100 (historic low prices right now)
- One 30A PWM charge controller: $15-30
- One 12V car battery (you probably have a spare, or pull one from a junk vehicle): $0-50
- Wire, connectors, fuses: $15-20
- **Total: $110-200**
- Output: 600-1,200 Wh per day (depends on season). That's lights, phones, radio, laptop — indefinitely.
- **This is the minimum viable solar system.** One panel, one battery, one controller. Expandable forever.

### IMPROVISED BATTERY SOURCES
When you can't buy batteries, where do you find 12V power?
- **Any vehicle** — car, truck, ATV, riding mower, boat. Each has a 12V battery.
- **UPS units** — every office has them. The batteries are usually 12V 7-9Ah sealed lead acid. 4 of them wired in parallel = a decent battery bank.
- **Old power tool batteries** — 18V/20V lithium. With a buck converter ($5), step down to 12V for charging.
- **Golf cart batteries** — deep-cycle 6V. Wire 2 in series = 12V. These are designed for cycling and last much longer than car batteries.
- **Electric scooter / e-bike batteries** — 36-72V lithium packs. Contain good cells. Can be disassembled and rewired.

### THE MICRO-HYDRO ADVANTAGE (North Idaho's secret weapon)

Most preppers think solar. Most preppers are wrong — at least in North Idaho.

**Solar in North Idaho winter:** 2-3 peak sun hours × 1kW array = 2-3 kWh/day. Pathetic.
**Micro-hydro in North Idaho winter:** 500W turbine × 24 hours = 12 kWh/day. Year-round. Night. Day. Snow. Rain. Doesn't matter.

A creek with 50 ft of head (drop) and 20 GPM flow produces ~500W continuous. That's more than enough for a cabin, a workshop, battery charging, and mesh radios. Creeks FLOW in winter in Idaho (they're spring/snowmelt fed). This is 4-6x more energy than solar in the worst months.

**Requirements:**
- Creek on or adjacent to your property
- Minimum: 5 GPM flow + 10 ft head for useful power (100W+)
- Sweet spot: 20+ GPM + 30-50 ft head = 300-500W continuous
- Pipe (penstock) to capture the head: 2-4" PVC, run from upstream intake to turbine
- PowerSpout PLT turbine: $2,000-3,500
- Total installed: $3,000-8,000
- **Legal:** Idaho requires a water right permit from IDWR even for small diversions. Run-of-river (no impoundment/dam) permits are simpler and faster.

## LAYER 6: EUREKA INNOVATIONS — FORTUNE 500 PITCH

### EUREKA 1: Pyrolysis — Turning Trash Into Fuel
**What:** Thermal decomposition of plastic waste at 400-500°C without oxygen. Output: diesel (~40-50% yield), gasoline (~20-30%), carbon black, syngas. Every community has plastic waste. Every community needs fuel. A small reactor (Beston, DOING Group) processes 100-500 kg/day for $8K-18K.
**The twist:** The syngas byproduct is hot enough to power the reactor itself. Once started, the process is self-sustaining — no external energy input. 1 ton of plastic = 400 kg of fuel oil. You're turning landfill into a gas station.
**Commander connection:** You run a diesel heater. Pyrolysis produces diesel. The loop: plastic waste → reactor → diesel → your heater → heat. Cost of fuel: $0.
**Legal:** No federal ban on producing fuel for personal use. EPA Clean Air Act applies to emissions. Idaho has no specific prohibition but you can't sell unrefined fuel commercially without permits.
**Why Fortune 500:** $9.9B pyrolysis market by 2030. Every municipality with a landfill problem is a customer.

### EUREKA 2: Second-Life EV Batteries — The 90% Discount Powerwall
**What:** 500,000 EV batteries will reach end-of-vehicle-life by 2030. Each contains thousands of cells. Most cells are fine — the pack failed because a few degraded. AI tests each cell in seconds (capacity, internal resistance), grades it, and matches it with identical cells. Result: battery packs that perform like new at 10% of new cost.
**The math:** Tesla Powerwall 3: $15,000+. DIY powerwall from salvaged Chevy Bolt modules: $1,500-2,500 for 14kWh. That's 85-90% savings for equivalent capacity.
**Sources:** BatteryHookup.com, eBay, secondlifestorage.com forums, local salvage yards. Chevy Bolt modules: $50-80/kWh. Tesla Model 3: $80-120/kWh.
**Equipment:** kWeld spot welder ($200), ZB206+ cell tester ($30), BMS boards ($50-150).
**Why Fortune 500:** $58B battery recycling market by 2031. Second-life batteries are the circular economy applied to the most expensive component in renewable energy.

### EUREKA 3: Micro-Hydro Networks — Creek-Powered Civilization
**What:** Connect multiple micro-hydro turbines across a valley's creek system. Each turbine feeds a local battery bank. Mesh radio monitors all turbines. AI load-balances: high-flow turbines charge community batteries; low-flow turbines serve local loads only. The network self-optimizes based on seasonal flow changes.
**The twist for North Idaho:** This area has hundreds of creeks running year-round. A coordinated micro-hydro network could power entire communities without a single solar panel or grid connection. 24/7, winter-proof, local, ungovernable. Each turbine is $2,000-3,500. A network of 10 = $20,000-35,000 total. That replaces a $2M grid substation.
**Why Fortune 500:** 1.2 billion people lack electricity. Micro-hydro works anywhere with flowing water + elevation change. The turbine is simple. The AI network is the innovation.

### EUREKA 4: Wood Gasification — Run a Generator on Trees
**What:** Wood chips/pellets heated in an oxygen-limited chamber produce syngas (carbon monoxide + hydrogen). Syngas runs a standard generator. All Power Labs PP20 Power Pallet: 15-20kW from wood chips. ~1 kg wood per kWh. A cord of wood = ~630 kWh.
**The twist for North Idaho:** Unlimited free wood (National Forest permit: $20 for 4 cords). A wood gasifier + generator turns forest deadfall into electricity. No fuel purchases ever. North Idaho has more wood energy per acre than almost anywhere in the lower 48.
**Cost:** PP20: ~$30K. DIY FEMA-style gasifier: $500-2,000. A cord of wood at $150 produces $63 worth of electricity at grid rates — but if the grid is down, that electricity is priceless.
**Why Fortune 500:** $4.7B wood heating market. Gasification converts low-value biomass into high-value electricity + heat.

### EUREKA 5: AI Energy Management — One Brain for Every Power Source
**What:** Solar, micro-hydro, generator, TEG, batteries, wood gasifier — most off-grid setups cobble these together with manual switching. AI manages all of them: predicts solar production from weather forecasts, monitors battery state, starts the generator only when needed, routes hydro power to highest-priority loads, tracks fuel consumption.
**The twist:** The AI LEARNS your energy patterns. It knows you use more power on Mondays (workshop day). It knows the creek drops in August. It knows your wood stove runs 14 hours/day in January (TEG production = 630 Wh). It pre-charges batteries before forecast clouds. It's the difference between manually managing 5 power sources and having a brain that does it.
**Why Fortune 500:** $34B energy management market. Every off-grid and hybrid-grid installation needs this. Every military forward operating base needs this. Every disaster relief deployment needs this.
