## NORTH IDAHO TRANSPORTATION REALITY

- **Distances are real** — Sandpoint to Spokane: 80 miles. Sandpoint to CDA: 45 miles. Nearest Costco: 45+ miles.
- **Winter shuts roads** — Ice, snow, avalanche zones on US-95. Schweitzer Mountain Road is brutal Nov-Apr.
- **One bridge across Lake Pend Oreille** (Long Bridge, US-95) — if it's down, the town splits in half
- **No public transit** — SPOT bus exists but is minimal. You need a vehicle. Period.
- **Fuel stations are sparse** — 3-4 in Sandpoint, then nothing for 30+ miles in some directions
- **Propane infrastructure is strong** — refill stations at every hardware store and gas station
- **Lake Pend Oreille is 43 miles long** — water transport connects communities that roads don't in winter
- **Railroad runs through town** — BNSF mainline. Not passenger, but the infrastructure exists.
- **Logging roads everywhere** — alternate routes through National Forest that don't appear on Google Maps
- **ATV/snowmobile culture is huge** — off-road access to properties is sometimes the ONLY winter access
- **Commander's situation:** Renting, needs mobile/flexible solutions, not permanent garage infrastructure

## LAYER 0: ZERO PREP — YOU HAVE NOTHING
### *Grid is down. Gas stations are closed. Roads may be blocked. How do you move?*

### OPTION A: YOUR FEET ($0)
**The most reliable transportation system ever invented.**
- Average human walks 3 mph. 15 miles/day is sustainable with a pack.
- Sandpoint to Hope: 15 miles. One day's walk along the lakeshore.
- **Footwear is everything.** The boots you're wearing right now determine your range. Quality hiking boots = 20+ miles. Sneakers = blisters at 5 miles.
- A walking stick (any hardwood branch) reduces joint stress by 25% and helps on ice.
- In snow: improvised snowshoes from bent saplings + paracord/wire. They work. Not pretty, but they work.

### OPTION B: BICYCLE ($0 if you own one)
**4x the range of walking for 1/3 the effort.**
- Average cyclist: 12-15 mph on roads. 50-60 miles/day is realistic with breaks.
- Sandpoint to CDA in a day. Sandpoint to Spokane in a hard day.
- **Mountain bikes are ideal for North Idaho** — gravel roads, logging roads, trail shortcuts that vehicles can't use.
- **Winter cycling is possible** with studded tires ($40-60/pair). The Finns do it at -20F.
- **Cargo capacity:** A rear rack + panniers carries 40-60 lbs. A bike trailer carries 100+ lbs.
- **Most common failure:** Flat tire. Carry a patch kit ($5) and a spare tube ($8). That's it.

### OPTION C: YOUR VEHICLE ON FUMES
**Most vehicles have 1-3 gallons when the light comes on. That's 30-60 miles.**
- **Rule:** Never let your tank go below half. This is 150-250 miles of emergency range.
- **Idle consumption:** A car at idle burns 0.15-0.5 gal/hour. If you're using your car as shelter/heat, turn it on 15 min/hour.
- **Hypermiling:** 45 mph in highest gear, windows up, AC off, gentle acceleration. Squeeze 30-40% more range.
- **Siphoning:** A $10 siphon pump transfers fuel from any vehicle to yours. In a real grid-down, abandoned vehicles are fuel sources.

### OPTION D: WATER ($0 if you're near the lake)
**Lake Pend Oreille connects communities that roads don't.**
- Any flat object that floats is a boat. A door. A pallet with garbage bags underneath. A log.
- 43 miles of shoreline communities. Sandpoint, Hope, Clark Fork, Bayview all connected by water.
- **Canoe/kayak:** No fuel needed. Human-powered. 3-4 mph sustained. A canoe carries 600+ lbs of cargo.
- **Winter:** The lake doesn't fully freeze (too deep, too much volume). This is actually an advantage — water transport works year-round.

### OPTION E: ANIMAL POWER ($0 if you know someone with horses)
**North Idaho has horses everywhere.**
- A horse covers 25-35 miles/day carrying a rider + 200 lbs.
- A pack horse carries 150-200 lbs without a rider.
- Horses navigate terrain that vehicles can't — snow trails, forest paths, steep grades.
- **Mules are better for pack work** — surer footing, less food required, harder to injure.
- This is not romantic or quaint. This is how the entire West was supplied for 200 years.

### PROPANE CONVERSION (The long-term fuel answer)

**Why propane:**
- Stores indefinitely (gasoline degrades in 3-12 months)
- 30-40% cheaper per gallon-equivalent
- Refill stations everywhere in Idaho
- Burns cleaner (longer engine life)
- A 100-gallon propane tank on a truck = 700+ miles of range

**Conversion options:**
- **ICOM/Prins bi-fuel kit:** Run on EITHER gasoline or propane. Switch with a button. $2,500-4,000 professional install.
- **DIY kits exist** but are tricky. Professional install recommended.
- **The play:** Convert to bi-fuel NOW. In a crisis, everyone fights over gasoline. You fill up at any propane exchange. The propane truck still runs when the gas truck stops.

### *The 5 things that make a Fortune 500 investor say "holy shit"*

### EUREKA 1: ARK Mobile — The Pi-Powered Vehicle Intelligence Platform
**What:** Pi 5 + OBD2 + GPS + 7" screen + LoRa mesh radio, mounted in any vehicle. Real-time diagnostics, navigation with offline maps, mesh communication, dash cam with AI object detection, fuel efficiency optimization. One $300 device replaces $2,000+ in aftermarket electronics AND works when the internet doesn't.
**Why it's Fortune 500:** $50B connected vehicle market. Every aftermarket electronics company is trying to build this. We build it from commodity hardware at 10% the cost.
**What we need:** Pi 5 (owned), OBD2 dongle ($20), GPS module ($15), 7" screen (ordered), LoRa module ($20), 3D-printed enclosure.

### EUREKA 2: Community E-Bike Sharing — Solar-Charged Fleet
**What:** 10 e-bikes stationed at solar charging hubs around town. Unlock with a mesh radio ping or simple code. $3/hour or $20/day. Solar panels charge bikes during the day. No app, no internet, no credit card processing needed — mesh-based unlock and simple cash/trade payment. In a grid-down scenario, these bikes are the only powered personal transport that works.
**Why it's Fortune 500:** $5B bike-sharing market. Nobody has done solar-charged, off-grid, mesh-unlocked e-bike sharing. Rural communities can't use Lime/Bird (no internet, no density). This fills the gap.
**What we need:** 10 e-bikes ($500-800 each converted), solar charging stations, mesh unlock system, simple accounting.

### EUREKA 3: Propane Vehicle Conversion Service — The Infinite Fuel Play
**What:** Become the regional propane conversion specialist. ICOM/Prins bi-fuel kits, professional install, guaranteed work. One shop doing 4 conversions/week at $3,000-4,000 each = $50K-65K/month revenue. Propane is already available everywhere. It stores indefinitely. It's 30-40% cheaper. The conversion pays for itself in 18-24 months on fuel savings alone — BEFORE any crisis pricing.
**Why it's Fortune 500:** $180B alternative fuel vehicle market. Propane is the unsexy answer that actually works TODAY, not in 10 years. CNG/LPG conversion companies are routinely acquired for 5-10x revenue.
**What we need:** Certified installer (training + certification), shop space, ICOM/Prins dealer agreement, first 5 showcase conversions.

### EUREKA 4: Lake Transport Network — Water Taxis for the Apocalypse
**What:** Lake Pend Oreille is 43 miles long and connects 6+ communities. Establish a scheduled water transport network — electric or propane-powered boats running Hope to Sandpoint to Clark Fork to Bayview. When roads are snowed in, when bridges are damaged, when fuel runs out — the lake still works. Add cargo capacity: move firewood, food, medical supplies by water.
**Why it's Fortune 500:** This is Venice meets rural America. Water transport is 10x more fuel-efficient than road per ton-mile. Every lakeside community in the world could use this model.
**What we need:** 2-3 boats (electric pontoon or converted propane), docking points, schedule, radio coordination.

### EUREKA 5: AI Route Optimization — Real-Time Alternate Paths
**What:** AI that knows every road, logging road, trail, and waterway in the region. Feed it real-time data: "Highway 95 blocked at mile marker 42. Snow depth 3 feet on Gold Hill Road. Lake is passable east of Green Monarch." AI generates the optimal route for your vehicle type, fuel level, and cargo. This is Waze for the wilderness — except it works without internet, runs on a Pi, and knows about the logging road that Google has never heard of.
**Why it's Fortune 500:** $30B navigation market. Google Maps doesn't know rural Idaho. Garmin doesn't update in real time. This is the layer between them.
**What we need:** OpenStreetMap data (offline), local road knowledge database, Pi 5 running route AI, Meshtastic for real-time road reports.
