## LAYER 0: ZERO PREP — YOU HAVE NOTHING
### *Grid is down. No internet. No phone. How do you access knowledge RIGHT NOW?*

### OPTION A: YOUR HEAD ($0)
**The only knowledge that's truly grid-proof is what you've memorized.**
- Can you start a fire without matches? Purify water? Tie 5 useful knots? Navigate by stars?
- Can you diagnose a car problem by sound? Identify 10 edible wild plants? Set a broken bone?
- **The gap between "I can Google that" and "I know that" is the gap between survival and not.**
- Start memorizing essential procedures NOW. Not all of them — just the ones that matter most:
  1. Water purification (3 methods)
  2. Fire starting (3 methods)
  3. Basic wound care (stop bleeding, prevent infection, splint)
  4. Shelter construction (emergency and long-term)
  5. Food preservation (drying, smoking, canning basics)

### OPTION B: BOOKS YOU ALREADY OWN ($0)
**Walk to your bookshelf. What's there?**
- Cookbooks = food preservation + nutrition knowledge
- Home repair manuals = shelter maintenance
- Vehicle manuals = transportation repair
- Garden books = food production
- First aid guides = medicine
- **The Haynes manual for your car is worth $500 in a grid-down scenario.**
- **Any Boy Scout handbook has more survival knowledge than most adults possess.**

### OPTION C: YOUR NEIGHBORS' KNOWLEDGE ($0)
**The 73-year-old rancher next door knows more useful information than the entire internet.**
- Who in your community can: weld? can food? deliver babies? set bones? build a fence? shoe a horse? repair an engine? wire a solar panel? identify medicinal plants?
- **Skill mapping is the single highest-value activity you can do for Layer 0.**
- Grab a notebook. Walk to 10 houses. Ask: "What can you do? What can you teach?"
- This IS your offline knowledge base.

### OPTION D: PRINTED INTERNET ($0 if you print it now)
**You have internet TODAY. Print the critical references BEFORE you need them.**
- **Priority print list:**
  1. First aid / wound care protocols (WebMD's first aid section, 20 pages)
  2. Water purification methods (EPA + CDC guidelines, 5 pages)
  3. Edible/medicinal plants of North Idaho (field guide format, 30 pages)
  4. Vehicle repair for your specific make/model (key procedures, 50 pages)
  5. Radio frequency charts (ham repeaters, FRS/GMRS channels, 2 pages)
  6. Idaho law essentials (self-defense, property, water rights, 10 pages)
  7. Gardening calendar for Zone 5b/6a (planting dates, 4 pages)
  8. Knot reference (8 essential knots with diagrams, 2 pages)
- **Laminate the critical ones.** A laminated reference card survives rain, mud, and blood.
