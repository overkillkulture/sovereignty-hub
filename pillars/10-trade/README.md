## LAYER 0: ZERO PREP — YOU HAVE NOTHING
### *Grid is down. Banks are closed. Cards don't work. How do you trade RIGHT NOW?*

### THE HIERARCHY OF POST-GRID CURRENCY (In order of acceptance)

**Tier 1: Immediate needs (first 72 hours)**
Everyone needs these. Everyone will trade for them.
- **Water** — even 1 gallon has trade value when taps stop
- **Food** — canned goods, dry goods, anything edible
- **Medicine** — OTC pain relievers, antibiotics, bandages
- **Fire** — lighters, matches, dry firewood
- **Information** — "the bridge is open" or "there's water at the church" has value

**Tier 2: Sustained crisis (week 1-4)**
Consumables with universal demand.
- **Ammunition** — .22LR, 9mm, .223/5.56. Divisible, durable, universally understood.
- **Alcohol** — sealed bottles. Liquor > beer (shelf life). Also disinfectant, trade lubricant.
- **Coffee and tobacco** — addiction commodities. People will trade disproportionately for these.
- **Fuel** — gasoline, propane, diesel. Measured value, immediate utility.
- **Batteries** — AA, AAA, D-cell. Power is power.
- **Hygiene** — soap, toilet paper, feminine products. Underrated trade items.

**Tier 3: Established crisis (month 1+)**
Durable goods and skills become dominant.
- **Tools** — hand tools, saws, axes, files, wrenches. Anyone with tools has leverage.
- **Seeds** — food production capability is the ultimate trade good.
- **Skills** — medical, mechanical, electrical, construction, food production. YOUR labor IS currency.
- **Precious metals** — silver coins, gold. Only valuable once immediate needs are met.

### HOW TO TRADE WHEN YOU HAVE NOTHING

**Trade your labor.**
- Split firewood for food. Fix a fence for fuel. Carry water for shelter.
- Everyone has labor. The question is: do you have SKILLED labor?
- **Top 5 skills by trade value:**
  1. Medical (stop bleeding, set bones, treat infection)
  2. Mechanical (fix engines, pumps, generators)
  3. Electrical (wire solar, fix wiring, build radio)
  4. Food production (garden, hunt, fish, preserve)
  5. Security (organized patrol, threat assessment)

**Trade information.**
- You walked 5 miles? You know which roads are open, where water is flowing, who needs what.
- Information is the only trade good that costs nothing to produce and doesn't diminish when shared.
- Be the person who KNOWS THINGS. That's trade capital.

### IMPROVISED MARKETPLACE ($0)
- **Community board:** Nail a piece of plywood to a post at a crossroads. People pin trade offers.
- **"Want/Have" lists:** Paper posted at gathering points. "HAVE: 20 lbs potatoes. WANT: AA batteries."
- **Voice marketplace:** Designate a radio channel (from Comms pillar) for trade: "Channel 2 = Trade."
- **Mesh marketplace:** If Meshtastic is running, text-based trade posts propagate automatically.

### JUNK SILVER (The post-dollar currency)

**What is junk silver?**
Pre-1965 US dimes, quarters, half dollars. 90% silver content. Recognizable, divisible, proven over 5,000 years.

| Coin | Silver Content | Current Price | Crisis Value |
|------|---------------|--------------|-------------|
| **Dime** (pre-1965) | 0.0723 oz silver | $2.50-3.50 | $5-15 |
| **Quarter** (pre-1965) | 0.1808 oz silver | $6-9 | $15-40 |
| **Half dollar** (pre-1965) | 0.3617 oz silver | $12-18 | $30-75 |
| **Silver spot price** | — | $28-33/oz (Apr 2026) | $75-200+/oz projected |

**Why junk silver beats gold:**
- Divisible (a dime buys a loaf of bread; a gold coin buys a truck)
- Recognizable (everyone knows what a US quarter looks like)
- Portable (a $100 face value bag = ~71.5 oz silver = fits in a shoebox)
- Verifiable (ring test, weight test, acid test — all simple)

**Where to buy:** JM Bullion, APMEX, SD Bullion online. Local coin shops in CDA and Spokane. Estate sales.

**How much to hold:** $100-500 face value ($2,000-10,000 at current premiums) gives you meaningful trade capability.

## LAYER 5: THE EUREKA INNOVATIONS
### *The 5 things that make a Fortune 500 investor say "holy shit"*

### EUREKA 1: Mesh-Based Digital Marketplace — LoRa Craigslist
**What:** Text-based marketplace running over Meshtastic mesh. No internet. No servers. No payment processors. "WTS: 20 lbs potatoes, $10, Node 7." "WTB: .22LR ammo, Node 12." Reputation system: "Node 7 has completed 23 trades, 100% positive." Disputes resolved by community arbitration (Governance pillar). This is Craigslist that works without electricity, internet, or centralized control.
**Why it's Fortune 500:** 3.7 billion people lack reliable internet. Every farmer in Africa, every village in Asia, every grid-down community everywhere needs markets. The protocol IS the platform.
**What we need:** Meshtastic channel protocol, Pi gateway for logging, reputation algorithm, text-format standard.

### EUREKA 2: Community Scrip — Local Currency Backed by Real Assets
**What:** Community-issued currency backed by ACTUAL LOCAL ASSETS. "1 Sandpoint Dollar = 1 gallon of clean water from the community well" or "1 SP$ = 1 kWh from the community solar array." Not fiat. Not crypto. Asset-backed, locally controlled, inflation-proof because the backing is physical and local. Legal under US law as long as it doesn't mimic US currency. Print on the Saturn 4 with embedded security features.
**Why it's Fortune 500:** $100T global currency market being decentralized. Community currencies (BerkShares, Ithaca Hours) have proven models. The platform to CREATE and MANAGE them is the product.
**What we need:** Legal framework, asset backing system, 3D-printed security features, community governance (Pillar 11), AI management.

### EUREKA 3: AI Trade Optimizer — Know What's Worth What
**What:** ARAYA monitors all trade activity across the mesh marketplace. Detects pricing patterns: "Firewood trades at 3:1 versus potatoes this week, up from 2:1 last week — firewood demand is rising." Predicts shortages: "Battery demand up 400% — acquire now before price doubles." Suggests optimal trades: "You have excess eggs. Node 14 has excess ammo and needs eggs. Suggested trade: 2 dozen eggs for 100 rounds .22LR." The AI is the invisible hand of the market made intelligent.
**Why it's Fortune 500:** $400B supply chain analytics market. This is Bloomberg Terminal for barter economies. Scales from 20 nodes to 200,000.
**What we need:** Trade data from mesh marketplace, Ollama on CP2, pricing models, recommendation engine.

### EUREKA 4: Huckleberry Futures — North Idaho's Cash Crop Goes Premium
**What:** Huckleberries grow wild in North Idaho, can't be commercially farmed, and sell for $30-40/gallon. Build a huckleberry brand: GPS-tagged harvest locations (verified wild), cold-chain tracking, premium packaging, online marketplace. "North Idaho Wild Huckleberries — GPS-verified, hand-picked, cold-shipped." Pre-sell harvest futures: "Reserve 5 gallons of 2027 harvest at $35/gallon, guaranteed." This is terroir marketing applied to a wild berry.
**Why it's Fortune 500:** $2B specialty berry market. Huckleberries are the truffle of North America — wild, scarce, premium. Brand + verification + futures = massive margins.
**What we need:** Harvest network (pickers), GPS tagging, cold chain, packaging, online store, futures contract system.

### EUREKA 5: Regional Trade Federation — Inter-Community Commerce Protocol
**What:** 10 communities across North Idaho, each with mesh-connected marketplaces, each with their own surpluses and deficits. Community A (lakeside) has fish. Community B (valley) has grain. Community C (mountain) has firewood. The federation protocol matches inter-community trades automatically. AI optimizes routing: "Truck from A to B carries fish, returns with grain, stops at C for firewood." One logistics network, many communities, AI-coordinated.
**Why it's Fortune 500:** This is the European Common Market at village scale. The protocol that connects local economies into regional ones is worth more than any single trade. This scales to any region on Earth.
**What we need:** 10 mesh-connected communities, standardized trade protocol, logistics coordination (Transportation pillar), AI matching, governance framework.
