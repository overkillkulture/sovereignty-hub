## NORTH IDAHO WATER REALITY

- **60% of water comes from snowmelt** — low-snow years = summer shortages
- **Ongoing drought since 2021** — classified across most basins
- **Dry season: July-September** — rain and snowmelt both gone. This is when you need storage.
- **Average rainfall: 25-30"/year** — heavier in winter/spring, almost nothing in summer
- **Most homes on wells** — electric submersible pumps that die when power goes out
- **Creek/river access everywhere** — Lake Pend Oreille, Pack River, Sand Creek
- **Water rights are real property rights in Idaho** — you need a permit from IDWR to divert creek water
- **Well depths vary wildly** — check yours at idwr.idaho.gov/wells/find-a-well-map/
- **Dec 2025 floods didn't help** — rain not snow, ran off instead of storing in snowpack
- **Planning implication:** You need 90 days of storage minimum for the dry season

## LAYER 0: ZERO PREP — YOU HAVE NOTHING
### *Grid is down. EMP. No prep. No tools. No money. How do you get water from your well RIGHT NOW?*

### OPTION A: THE 2-LITER BAILER ($0, 10 minutes)
**You need:** A 2-liter soda bottle + paracord or any rope.
**How:** Tie rope to bottle neck. Remove well cap (usually 4 bolts or a compression fit). Lower bottle into well casing. It fills from the open top. Pull up slowly so it doesn't tip. Pour into bucket. Repeat. ~0.5 gallons per trip. Tedious but you will not die of thirst.
**Limits:** Works in any 4"+ well casing. No depth limit (just need enough rope). Slow.

### OPTION B: THE DIY WELL BAILER ($10-15, 30 minutes)
**You need:** 20" of 3.5" PVC pipe (fits inside 4" casing), a ball check valve OR a glass marble + rubber hose washer + PVC cap, an eyebolt, rope.
**How:** Check valve goes on bottom. Eyebolt goes on top for rope. Lower into well. Water pushes check valve open and fills pipe. Pull up — check valve seals, water stays in. Pour into bucket. 1.5-2 gallons per trip.
**Where to get parts:** Any hardware store. Lowes, Home Depot, Ace. All basic plumbing aisle.
**Pro version:** Lehman's sells a galvanized well bucket for 4" wells, 1.9 gallons. ~$60.

### OPTION C: THE DIY PVC HAND PUMP — "HOLOPUMP" ($30-50, few hours)
**This is the real answer. A permanent hand pump from hardware store PVC.**
**Parts list:**
- 1-1/4" PVC pipe (riser, length = well depth + 5 feet above ground)
- 3/4" PVC rod (piston rod, same length)
- O-rings for piston seal
- 2 check valves (one at bottom, one on piston — or make them from marbles + rubber washers, ~$3)
- T-fitting for handle
- PVC cement, pipe clamps
- Total: **$30-50 in hardware store parts**

**Performance:** Pumps reliably from **60 feet**, tested to 80 ft with 20-ft pipe sections. ~2 gallons per minute with steady pumping. Installs alongside existing electric submersible.
**Build guides:**
- Mother Earth News "Holopump": motherearthnews.com/diy/pvc-manual-well-pump-zmaz00jjzgoe/
- Instructables "O-Ringer" pump: instructables.com/Make-an-O-ringer-PVC-Manual-Lift-Pump-for-Your-Wel/
- YouTube: "Emergency Well Pump Requires No Electricity" (search this exact title)

**PRINT THESE INSTRUCTIONS NOW. LAMINATE THEM. STORE IN YOUR WELL HOUSE.**

### OPTION D: CAR BATTERY + 12V SUBMERSIBLE PUMP ($80-120)
**If you have a running vehicle, you have power.**
- ECO-WORTHY or VEVOR 12V DC submersible pump: $80-120 on Amazon
- 3" diameter (fits 4" well casing)
- Pumps from up to **98 feet submersion**, 3.2 GPM
- Connect to car battery with alligator clips. Draws 10-45 amps.
- A car battery runs it for **2-4 hours** (idle car to recharge)
- **EMP NOTE:** Simple brushed DC motors have no circuit boards — likely survives EMP. Store one in an ammo can (Faraday cage) as insurance.

### OPTION E: SURFACE WATER (No well needed)
**North Idaho has creeks, rivers, and lakes everywhere.**
- Lake Pend Oreille, Pack River, Sand Creek, countless streams
- **Collection:** Any container. 5-gallon bucket in the creek. Tarp spread to catch rain.
- **Treatment:** Boil for 1 minute (rolling boil). Or Sawyer Squeeze ($45). Or 2 drops bleach per quart (let sit 30 min).
- **WARNING:** Idaho water rights law requires a permit to divert creek water. In a true emergency nobody is enforcing this, but know it exists.

### IMPROVISED RAIN COLLECTION (No equipment needed)
- **Tarp method:** Spread any tarp, plastic sheet, or garbage bags on the ground or angled against a wall. Funnel low point into a bucket. 1" of rain on a 10x10 tarp = ~6 gallons.
- **Roof runoff:** Find any gutter downspout. Place a bucket under it. If no gutters, lean a board or sheet metal against the drip line to channel water.
- **Vehicle method:** Open the trunk, line it with a trash bag, angle the car on a slope. Windshield and roof collect rain into the trunk.
- **Winter:** 10 inches of snow = ~1 inch of water. Fill a black trash bag with snow, set it in the sun or near any heat source. Melt, filter, drink.

### EMP SURVIVAL NOTES
| Equipment | Survives EMP? | Notes |
|-----------|--------------|-------|
| Solar panels (bare) | **YES** | Simple silicon, no microelectronics |
| Charge controllers / inverters | **NO** | MOSFET/IGBT circuits, highly vulnerable |
| Submersible well pump (AC motor) | **Probably yes** if disconnected | Simple motor windings |
| 12V DC submersible pump | **Likely yes** | Brushed DC motors, no controller board |
| VFD/variable speed controllers | **NO** | Solid state, dead |
| Manual hand pumps | **YES** | No electronics whatsoever |
| **Mitigation:** Store a spare charge controller + 12V pump in a metal ammo can (Faraday cage). RPS Solar Pumps sells a spare controller in a Faraday bag specifically for this. |

### STORAGE MATH

```
1 person  = 1 gal/day drinking + 1 gal/day cooking/hygiene = 2 gal/day
4 people  = 8 gal/day
30 days   = 240 gallons minimum
90 days   = 720 gallons (dry season coverage)

1 IBC tote (275 gal) + 2 drums (110 gal) + WaterBOB (100 gal) = 485 gallons
That's 60 days for a family of 4. Add rain collection and you're covered.
```

### WINTERIZATION (North Idaho specific)
- Rain barrels MUST be drained before first hard freeze (typically mid-October)
- IBC totes in unheated spaces will freeze — insulate or move to garage
- Keep 30 days of stored water INDOORS year-round as cold-weather baseline
- Snow = water. 10 inches of snow = ~1 inch of water. Melt it, filter it.
- Snowmelt collection: a clean tarp + gravity funnel into a bucket = free water all winter

## LAYER 5: THE EUREKA INNOVATIONS
### *The 5 things that make a Fortune 500 investor say "holy shit"*

### EUREKA 1: Atmospheric Water Generation + AI Optimization
**What:** A machine that pulls drinking water from humidity. Existing AWGs ($1,500-30,000) are dumb — fixed settings, wasted energy. Add AI that reads local humidity, temperature, dew point, and consumption patterns. Same hardware produces 40% more water for 30% less energy. Network AWGs across a town. AI load-balances: surplus units throttle, deficit units ramp. Self-optimizing water grid.
**Why it's Fortune 500:** $280B global water treatment market. 2.2 billion people lack safe water. This scales to any climate.
**What we need:** AWG unit ($1,500+ entry), Pi 5 running monitoring AI, humidity/temp sensors, mesh radio for networking.

### EUREKA 2: Graphene Oxide Nano-Filtration
**What:** Graphene oxide membranes filter at the molecular level — heavy metals, pharmaceuticals, microplastics, PFAS "forever chemicals" that ceramic and carbon can't touch. Entering commercial production now. A graphene cartridge in a gravity filter system turns a $50 bucket setup into something that outperforms a $50,000 municipal treatment stage.
**Why it's Fortune 500:** PFAS contamination is a national crisis. Every military base, every industrial town needs this. The market is just opening.
**What we need:** Source graphene oxide filter cartridges (commercial suppliers entering market 2025-2026), design our own gravity housing, test and certify.

### EUREKA 3: Solar Desalination at Household Scale
**What:** Passive solar stills that produce 5+ liters/hour using only sunlight. MIT demonstrated this. No electricity. A suitcase-sized device turns saltwater or contaminated water into drinking water. For coastal/arid communities: unlimited water from the ocean powered by the sun. With 3D-printed components: under $500 per unit.
**Why it's Fortune 500:** 40% of the world's population lives within 100km of a coast. This is the unlock.
**What we need:** Solar still design (open source), 3D-printed components (Saturn 4), glass/polycarbonate cover, basin.

### EUREKA 4: AI Water Quality Network — The Grassroots EPA
**What:** 50 communities with $40 TDS meters + bacteria tests + AI logging = the most comprehensive real-time water quality dataset in the country. AI detects patterns no human could: "Nitrate spikes in agricultural zones every March-May." "Lead correlates with construction on Pine Street." "Community 23's well shows upstream contamination trending." You accidentally build a grassroots EPA — faster, more granular, more current than the real one.
**Why it's Fortune 500:** Water quality data is worth billions. Municipalities will pay for this data. Insurance companies will pay for this data. The network IS the product.
**What we need:** TDS meters ($8 each), bacteria test kits ($10-15), mesh radio reporting, Pi 5 running AI analysis, database.

### EUREKA 5: Closed-Loop Greywater Recycling → Aquaponics
**What:** Shower/sink water (greywater) → biofilter (sand + gravel + plants) → aquaponics system (Food pillar). Fish clean it. Plants clean it. Output water is cleaner than input. A household goes from 80 gal/day fresh input to 20 gal/day because the loop recycles 75%. AI monitors chemistry and adjusts flow. Water becomes renewable, not consumable.
**Why it's Fortune 500:** Water scarcity + food production in one system. This is the circular economy applied to the two most basic human needs.
**What we need:** Greywater diverter ($50-100), biofilter materials ($30), plumbing to aquaponics, AI chemistry monitoring.
