## NORTH IDAHO FOOD REALITY

- **Growing season: ~120-150 days** (last frost mid-May, first frost mid-September)
- **Winter: 8-9 hours of low-angle daylight** — indoor growing without supplemental light is marginal
- **Rich foraging:** Huckleberries, morels, serviceberries, cattails, dandelion, wild onion, pine nuts
- **Wild game:** Deer, elk, turkey, grouse, fish (Lake Pend Oreille, rivers)
- **Soil:** Generally acidic, rocky in many areas. Raised beds are often easier than ground planting.
- **Water for growing:** Depends on Pillar 1. Aquaponics recycles 90% of water.
- **Key constraint:** The winter. You MUST have indoor/preserved food capability for November through April.
- **Key advantage:** Long summer days (15-16 hours of light in June) mean explosive summer growth

## LAYER 0: ZERO PREP — YOU HAVE NOTHING
### *Grid is down. Grocery stores are empty. What do you eat TODAY?*

### HOUR 1: YOUR PANTRY
Before you go outside, inventory what you have. Most households have 3-14 days of food they don't realize:
- **Dry goods:** Rice, pasta, beans, oats, flour — these are calorie-dense and last
- **Canned goods:** Vegetables, soups, tuna, chicken — eat these first (they're heavy to move if you have to bug out)
- **Condiments as survival food:** Peanut butter (190 cal/serving, lasts 2 years open), honey (never expires, pure energy), olive oil (120 cal/tablespoon — the most calorie-dense thing in your kitchen)
- **Freezer race:** Your freezer stays cold ~24-48 hours if you don't open it. After that, cook everything and eat or smoke/dry it before it spoils.

### HOUR 2-24: SPROUTING FROM YOUR PANTRY
**You can sprout almost any dried bean or lentil from your pantry RIGHT NOW.**
- Take dried lentils, mung beans, or chickpeas from your shelf
- Rinse. Soak overnight in a jar with water
- Drain. Rinse 2x per day. Cover jar with cloth/screen
- **Edible sprouts in 2-3 days.** No soil. No light. No electricity.
- Nutrition explodes during germination: 4-10x vitamin increase (especially Vitamin C)
- **Calorie warning:** Sprouts are nutrient-dense but calorie-poor (~30 cal/cup). They supplement stored grains, they don't replace them.

### DAY 1-3: FORAGING (North Idaho specific)
**What's edible within walking distance of most North Idaho properties:**

| Plant | Season | Where to Find | What to Eat | Calories |
|-------|--------|--------------|-------------|----------|
| **Dandelion** | Year-round (greens), spring best | Every lawn, field, roadside | Leaves (salad/cooked), roots (roast for coffee), flowers (fritters) | Low cal, high vitamin A, C, K |
| **Cattail** | Year-round | Any pond, marsh, ditch, lakeshore | Roots (starch, peel and boil/roast), shoots (like asparagus), pollen (flour substitute) | Roots are calorie-dense: ~270 cal/100g |
| **Pine nuts** | Fall | Whitebark pine, higher elevations | Crack shells, eat raw or roast | Very high: ~670 cal/100g |
| **Wild onion/ramps** | Spring | Moist forest floors, streambanks | Bulb and greens, use like onion/garlic | Low cal but essential nutrients |
| **Miner's lettuce** | Spring-early summer | Shady moist areas, forest edges | Leaves raw or cooked, mild flavor | Low cal, high vitamin C |
| **Huckleberries** | Mid-June through August | Higher elevations, burned areas | Eat fresh, dry for storage | ~60 cal/cup |
| **Morel mushrooms** | Spring (April-June) | After forest fires, disturbed soil, near cottonwood/aspen | Cook thoroughly — never eat raw | ~30 cal/cup but high protein |
| **Serviceberries** | June-July | Forest edges, streambanks | Eat fresh, dry like raisins | ~80 cal/cup |
| **Thimbleberries** | July-August | Forest clearings, roads | Eat fresh (don't store well) | ~50 cal/cup |

**CRITICAL WARNINGS:**
- **Death camas** looks like wild onion but is DEADLY. Wild onion smells like onion. Death camas does NOT. Smell it first. Always.
- **False morels** (Gyromitra) are poisonous. True morels are hollow when cut in half. False morels are solid or cottony inside. Cut every morel in half before eating.
- **Field guides for North Idaho:** *Practical Guide to Northern Idaho's Edible Wild Plants* by Steven Golieb ($15), *Foraging Idaho* by Christopher Nyerges ($22). **Buy. Print. Laminate. Put in your go-bag.**

### DAY 1-7: FISHING AND TRAPPING
- **Fishing:** Handline kit ($10) or improvised line + hook. Lake Pend Oreille has lake trout, rainbow trout, kokanee, bass, perch. Pack River and Sand Creek are accessible on foot.
- **Snare wire:** 10 ft of brass snare wire ($5) catches rabbits and squirrels. Set on game trails at dusk.
- **Idaho hunting/fishing regs:** In a true grid-down emergency, regulations become secondary to survival. But know: you need a license in normal times.

### THE CALORIE MATH (How much do you actually need?)
```
Sedentary adult: ~2,000 cal/day
Active (building, hauling water, chopping wood): ~3,000-3,500 cal/day
Family of 4 (mixed activity): ~10,000 cal/day

25 lb bag of rice = ~40,000 calories = 4 days for a family of 4
25 lb bag of dried beans = ~38,000 calories = 3.8 days
1 gallon olive oil = ~31,000 calories = 3 days
5 lb peanut butter = ~13,000 calories = 1.3 days

REALITY: A family of 4 doing physical work burns through
a 25 lb bag of rice in 4 DAYS. Plan accordingly.
```

## LAYER 6: EUREKA INNOVATIONS — FORTUNE 500 PITCH
### *The 5 technologies that make investors say "holy shit"*

### EUREKA 1: Aeroponic Semi Trailers — Farm on Wheels
**What:** A 40-ft semi trailer converted to an aeroponic growing system. Roots hang in air, mist delivers nutrients, AI optimizes growing cycles. Freight Farms Greenery S grows 4,000 heads of lettuce/month in 320 sq ft. GrowTrucks builds purpose-built trailer systems. DIY conversion: used trailer ($3,000-5,000) + hydro system + LEDs = $15,000-30,000.
**The twist:** The trailer is MOBILE. Drive it to a disaster zone. Park it. Growing food in 48 hours. Drive it to a food desert. Park it. A farmer's market that grows its own inventory. Drive it to a military base. Park it. Fresh food for 200 soldiers.
**Power:** 125-268 kWh/day. Solar-viable with ~30-40kW of panels ($60-80K installed). Or run from any generator.
**Why Fortune 500:** Food security as a deployable asset. FEMA, military, municipalities, corporations all need this.

### EUREKA 2: AI Plant Health — $15 Camera Replaces $50K Agronomist
**What:** A $15 camera module on a rail scans plants. AI reads leaf color, stem angle, growth rate, detects disease 3-5 days before human eyes can see it. Open-source models exist (AgroVisionNet, MobileViT). Runs on a Raspberry Pi.
**The twist:** The AI learns YOUR specific microclimate, YOUR water chemistry, YOUR seed genetics. Every harvest trains the next one. After a year, the system knows your growing conditions better than any human expert. And it shares anonymized data across the network — every farm makes every other farm smarter.
**Why Fortune 500:** $12B vertical farming market. Biggest cost is labor for monitoring. AI cuts that to near zero.

### EUREKA 3: Mycelium Farming — Grow Protein in the Dark
**What:** Mushrooms grow in the dark, on agricultural waste (coffee grounds, straw, wood chips), in 2-3 weeks. No sunlight. No soil. No growing season. Oyster mushrooms produce 1 lb per square foot every 2 weeks. A 10x10 room produces 50 lbs/week year-round.
**The twist:** Mycelium isn't just food. Mycelium-based materials replace leather, styrofoam, insulation, and building materials. You're not growing mushrooms — you're growing a materials platform that ALSO produces food. Companies like Ecovative and MyForest Foods are valued at $100M+.
**Why Fortune 500:** Mushrooms are the fastest-growing protein category. Mycelium materials are replacing petroleum-based products. Two markets, one organism.

### EUREKA 4: Insect Protein — Black Soldier Fly Larvae
**What:** Black soldier fly larvae (BSFL) convert food waste into high-protein animal feed (42% protein, 35% fat). They self-harvest by crawling up a ramp when ready to pupate. Zero human labor at harvest. They eat ANYTHING organic — kitchen scraps, manure, spoiled food. A 4x4 ft bin processes 5 lbs of food waste per day and produces 1 lb of larvae.
**The twist:** The larvae ARE the feed for your chickens, quail, fish (aquaponics), and rabbits. Your food waste becomes your animal feed. The loop closes. And the frass (larvae poop) is premium fertilizer. Waste → protein → more food → waste → protein. Infinite.
**Why Fortune 500:** $1.3B insect protein market by 2030 (25% CAGR). Largest application: animal feed replacement for soy and fishmeal.

### EUREKA 5: AI Food Forest Design — Permaculture by Algorithm
**What:** Describe your lot: size, soil type, sunlight hours, water access, climate zone, slope, existing trees. AI generates a permaculture food forest design — what to plant where, companion planting, succession planting, harvest calendar, yield projections. A design that would cost $2,000-5,000 from a permaculture consultant, generated in minutes.
**The twist:** The AI designs for 30-year yields, not just this season. It places nut trees that won't bear for 5 years in positions that provide windbreak NOW. It interplants nitrogen fixers with heavy feeders. It knows that a black walnut tree kills tomatoes within 50 feet (juglone toxicity) but cherries thrive beside it. No human remembers all 10,000 companion planting rules. AI does.
**Why Fortune 500:** $350B global agriculture technology market. Every homeowner with a yard is a customer. Every community with unused land is a customer.
