## LAYER 0: ZERO PREP — YOU HAVE NOTHING
### *Someone is hurt. No hospital. No ambulance. No phone. What do you do?*

### THE 3-MINUTE PRIORITIES (MARCH protocol)
```
M — Massive hemorrhage (stop the bleeding FIRST — tourniquet, direct pressure)
A — Airway (tilt head, lift chin, clear obstruction)
R — Respiration (look for chest wounds, seal any holes)
C — Circulation (check pulse, treat for shock — elevate legs, keep warm)
H — Hypothermia/Head injury (prevent heat loss, monitor consciousness)
```

### STOP THE BLEEDING WITH NOTHING
- **Direct pressure:** Your hand. Your shirt. Anything clean pushed hard into the wound. Don't lift to check — add more layers on top.
- **Improvised tourniquet:** Belt, strip of fabric, paracord — wrap 2-3 inches above the wound, tighten with a stick (windlass). Twist until bleeding stops. Note the time. Tourniquet stays on until professional care.
- **Elevation:** Raise the wound above the heart. Gravity reduces blood flow.
- **Pressure points:** Brachial artery (inner upper arm) for arm wounds. Femoral artery (groin crease) for leg wounds. Press HARD against the bone.
