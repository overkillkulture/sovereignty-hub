## LAYER 0: ZERO PREP — YOU HAVE NOTHING
### *Grid is down. Supply chains broken. You need a part that doesn't exist. What do you do?*

### IMPROVISED MANUFACTURING (No power tools)

**Hand carving / whittling:**
- A knife and a piece of wood = handles, pegs, utensils, brackets, wedges, toggles
- Replacement tool handles: axe, hammer, shovel, rake — all are just shaped wood
- Wooden pegs replace nails/screws in a pinch (traditional timber framing used zero metal)
- A spokeshave ($15) and a drawknife ($20-40) turn a log into a tool handle in 30 minutes

**Metal bending / cold forming:**
- A vise ($30-60) + hammer + anvil (any heavy flat steel — railroad track section, I-beam cutoff) = bend, flatten, shape any mild steel
- Rebar bends into hooks, brackets, hinges, handles, grates
- Tin cans cut and formed into patches, shims, funnels, stove pipe, cups, containers
- Wire (fencing, baling, coat hangers) bends into springs, clips, hangers, snare loops

**Casting / molding:**
- Sand casting: pack wet sand around a pattern, remove pattern, pour molten aluminum (melts at 1,220°F — a wood fire with bellows reaches this)
- Source aluminum: beer/soda cans (free, everywhere), old engine parts, scrap
- A backyard forge from a brake drum + hair dryer (bellows) + charcoal melts aluminum in minutes
- Cast: replacement parts, brackets, hinges, pulleys, weights, cookware

**Clay / ceramics:**
- Clay soil is everywhere in North Idaho. Dig it, clean it, shape it, fire it in a pit fire (1,400-1,800°F)
- Water filter elements (basic ceramic), pots, containers, pipe fittings, stove parts, bricks
- A basic kick-wheel (hand-built from a car brake rotor + bearing + wood) = pottery production

### THE REPAIR ECONOMY
In a grid-down scenario, the most valuable manufacturing skill is **REPAIR, not creation.**
- The person who can fix a broken pump handle is worth more than the person who can build one from scratch
- Every house has 10+ broken things. Each fix is a trade or a sale.
- **Essential repair supplies to have:** JB Weld ($5-8), electrical tape, wire, assorted screws/nuts/bolts, zip ties, hose clamps, duct tape, PVC cement, solder + flux, super glue, epoxy, Sugru ($10)
- A $50 assorted repair kit becomes a mobile repair business in a crisis

### THE FILAMENT INSIGHT
3D printer filament is the 21st century equivalent of raw metal ingots. A 1 kg spool of PETG ($15-20 today) becomes 50-200 printed parts worth $5-20 each = $250-4,000 in finished goods. That's a 15-250x multiplier on raw material cost. In a crisis, the person with filament and a working printer is a factory.

## LAYER 6: EUREKA INNOVATIONS — FORTUNE 500 PITCH

### EUREKA 1: Voice-to-Object Manufacturing
**What:** Describe what you need out loud. "I need a bracket that holds a 2-inch pipe to a flat wall at 45 degrees." AI generates the 3D model in seconds. Slicer prepares it. Printer builds it. Voice → object in under an hour.
**The twist:** This already works with GPT-4 + OpenSCAD. You describe in natural language, AI writes the parametric CAD code, renders it, exports STL. The gap is reliability and user interface — but it's closing fast. Within 2 years, voice-to-print will be as routine as voice-to-text.
**Why Fortune 500:** $83.4B additive manufacturing market by 2030 (35% CAGR). Voice-to-object removes the #1 barrier: CAD skills.

### EUREKA 2: Self-Replicating Printer Farms
**What:** A RepRap-based FDM printer prints 60%+ of its own parts. The non-printable parts (motors, electronics, rods) cost ~$40. One printer becomes two. Two become four. Four become eight. A factory that builds factories.
**The twist:** Network them. AI manages the print queue across all printers. Customer jobs distribute automatically. Printers that finish early help printers that are behind. One person manages a fleet of 20 printers from a phone.
**Why Fortune 500:** Decentralized manufacturing. Every community becomes a factory. No supply chain needed — just filament and electricity.

### EUREKA 3: AI Parts Identification — Photo to Replacement
**What:** Customer photographs a broken part. AI identifies it: dimensions, material, function, tolerances. AI generates the replacement model. Printer builds it. The customer doesn't need to know CAD, engineering, or even the name of the part.
**The twist:** Build a database of every replacement part ever printed. Each job trains the system. After 10,000 parts, the AI recognizes most common failures instantly: "That's a dishwasher door latch, model KitchenAid KDTM354DSS, I have the STL on file." Amazon ships in 3-5 days. You print in 30 minutes.
**Why Fortune 500:** The right-to-repair movement is becoming law. Manufacturers don't want to sell replacement parts. You become the source.

### EUREKA 4: CNC + 3D Print Hybrid Manufacturing
**What:** CNC router cuts structural parts from plywood/aluminum (strong, fast). 3D printer produces complex geometry parts (joints, brackets, housings). Together: a hybrid manufacturing system that builds almost anything. Houses (WikiHouse panels). Furniture (CNC-cut flat-pack). Electronics enclosures (3D print). Vehicle parts (CNC aluminum + 3D print housings).
**The twist:** Both machines run from the same AI. One design file generates both CNC tool paths and 3D print files. The AI decides which parts are CNC jobs and which are print jobs based on material, strength, and cost optimization.
**Why Fortune 500:** Distributed manufacturing is the future. CNC + 3D print + AI = a factory in every community.

### EUREKA 5: The Maker Hub Franchise — Factory in a Box
**What:** A complete, turnkey manufacturing business: 1 resin printer + 1 FDM printer + 1 CNC router + welding station + tool library + STL/file library + training + ARAYA AI managing the queue. Ship it in a crate. Set it up in a garage. You're a factory.
**The twist:** Shared STL library across all franchise locations. Design once, print everywhere. A franchise in Idaho designs a better water filter housing. It's instantly available to every franchise globally. The network effect makes every maker hub smarter.
**Why Fortune 500:** This is the McDonald's of manufacturing. Standardized, replicable, scalable. Each unit generates $40,000-75,000/year in a small community.
